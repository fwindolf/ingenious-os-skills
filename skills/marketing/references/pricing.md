# Pricing Strategy

Pricing decisions, tier structure, and pricing page for ingeniOS.

## Three Axes

1. **Packaging**: Which features in which tier
2. **Pricing Metric**: Per user, per usage, flat fee
3. **Price Point**: Actual amounts

## Value-Based Pricing

Customer's perceived value (ceiling) > Your price > Next best alternative (floor) > Cost to serve

## Value Metrics

Choose where more use = more value:
- Per Mitarbeiter (natural for time tracking — more employees = more value)
- Per Betrieb (flat fee — simple, predictable)
- Per feature module (invoicing, time tracking, projects)

## Good-Better-Best

| Tier | Target | Features | Price Anchor |
|------|--------|----------|-------------|
| Starter | Solo-Meister, 1-2 people | Core invoicing + time tracking | Low |
| Professional | Small Betrieb, 3-10 people | All features, DATEV export | Recommended |
| Business | Growing Betrieb, 10+ people | Everything + priority support | 2-3x Professional |

Differentiate by: feature gating, usage limits (invoices/month, employees), support level.

## Freemium vs Free Trial

For ingeniOS context:
- **Free trial** (14-30 days) likely better: product needs time to show value, B2B decision process, higher price point
- Consider: full access trial, no credit card upfront (lower barrier for Handwerker)
- Conversion expectation: 15-25% without CC upfront, 40-50% with CC

## Pricing Page

- Comparison table with feature rows
- Recommended tier highlighted
- Monthly/annual toggle (17-20% annual discount)
- FAQ addressing price objections
- "Kostenlos testen" CTA
- Customer logos when available

## Research Methods

**Van Westendorp** (4 questions):
1. At what price too expensive?
2. At what price expensive but acceptable?
3. At what price a bargain?
4. At what price too cheap (quality concern)?

Plot curves → find optimal price range.

## Handwerk Context

- Competitors: Lexware ~€30-50/month, Craftnote ~€30-40/user, Plancraft ~€30-50/user
- Handwerker are price-sensitive but will pay for something that clearly saves time
- Position against the cost of the current Zettelwirtschaft, not just competitors
- Annual billing preferred (Steuerberater can expense it)
