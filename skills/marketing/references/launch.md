# Launch Strategy

Planning product launches and feature releases for ingeniOS.

## ORB Framework

- **Owned**: Email list, blog, community, product changelog
- **Rented**: Social media (LinkedIn, Instagram), app stores, YouTube
- **Borrowed**: Guest content, collaborations, trade publications, Innungen events

## Five-Phase Launch

### Phase 1: Internal
- Early users, friendly feedback, fix critical issues
- No public marketing

### Phase 2: Alpha
- Landing page live, beta signup form
- Start collecting emails
- Soft outreach to known contacts

### Phase 3: Beta
- Expand early access (target: first 10 Betriebe)
- Collect testimonials and case studies
- Refine messaging based on real user language

### Phase 4: Early Access
- Broader invitations, generate buzz
- Gather data for social proof
- Refine pricing and packaging
- Create comparison pages, feature pages

### Phase 5: Full Launch
- Open signups, charge for the product
- Announce everywhere: trade publications, social, email, Innungen
- Product Hunt if relevant (secondary — primary audience is German)

## Post-Launch

- Onboarding email sequence for new signups
- Roundup email summarizing launch
- Comparison pages (vs Craftnote, vs Plancraft, etc.)
- Interactive demo or guided tour

## Ongoing Releases

- **Major**: Full campaign (blog + email + social + outreach)
- **Medium**: Blog post + email to existing users
- **Minor**: Changelog entry

## ingeniOS-Specific Notes

- Primary channel: direct outreach to Handwerk businesses (not PLG-first)
- Trade publications (SBZ, IKZ) carry weight in this market
- Innungen events and Handwerkskammer connections matter
- German language everywhere
- Focus on trust signals: DSGVO, German hosting, GoBD compliance
